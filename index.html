      I direct my Trustee to hold that property on the following trusts:

      Debts to Be Paid from My Estate

      1. to pay out of my estate:
        1. my debts, including income taxes payable up to and including the date of my death;
        2. my funeral and other expenses related to this Will and my death;
        3. all estate, gift, inheritance, succession, and other death taxes or duties payable in respect of all property passing on my death, including:
          1. insurance proceeds on my life payable as a consequence of my death (but excluding the proceeds of insurance upon my life owned by any corporation or owned by any partnership of which I am a partner);
          2. any annuity, registered retirement savings plan, registered retirement income fund, pension, or superannuation benefits payable to any person as a result of my death;
          3. any gift made by me in my lifetime; and
          4. any benefit arising by survivorship,

          and my Trustee may pay these taxes whether they are imposed by the law of this jurisdiction or any other and my Trustee may prepay or delay payment of any taxes or duties.

        Gift of Articles

      2. if Mary is not alive on the date that is 30 days after the date of my death:
        1. to distribute those items of the Articles that remain (the “Articles”) as equally as is reasonably practicable among those of my son Henry James Smith (“Hank”), my daughter Susan Mary Smith (“Susan”), and my daughter Elizabeth Anne Smith (“Liz”) who are alive on the date that is 5 days after the date of my death;
        2. it is my hope that my Trustee will, together with my children, devise a format of division of the Remaining Articles that results in those of Hank, Susan, and Liz who are alive on the date that is 5 days after the date of my death, receiving items (or items and cash, if an equalizing payment is required) of approximately the same total market value;
        3. to pay all of the packing, freight, and insurance costs my Trustee decides is appropriate for delivering any items of the Articles as required by this Will;

        3. Gift to Charity

      3. to pay $2,000 to ABC Foundation (“ABC Foundation”) of 567 Elsewhere Street, Othertown, British Columbia to be applied by the ABC Foundation for its education programs;

        4. Residue of Estate

      4. to give the residue of my estate to Mary, if she is alive on the date that is 30 days after the date of my death;
      5. if Mary is not alive on the date that is 30 days after the date of my death, to divide the residue of my estate into as many equal shares as there are of my children who are alive on the date that is 30 days after the date of my death, except if any child of mine has died before that date and one or more of his or her children are alive on that date, that deceased child will be considered alive for the purposes of the division, and:
        1. hold one of those shares for each child of mine who is alive on that date, and:
          1. pay or use so much of the income and capital of that child’s share as my Trustee decides is necessary or advisable for the care, maintenance, education, and benefit of that child until he or she dies or reaches 25;
          2. add any income not paid or used in any year to the capital of that child’s share;
          3. when that child reaches 21, give that child one-half of what remains of his or her share;
          4. when that child reaches 25, give that child what remains of that child’s share;
          5. if that child dies before reaching 25 and leaves one or more children alive on the date that is one day after the date of that child’s death, divide what remains of that child’s share equally among those of that child’s children who are alive on that date;
          6. if that child dies before reaching 25 and leaves no child alive on the date that is one day after the date of that child’s death, divide what remains of that child’s share equally among the other shares created under the provisions of paragraph 7(e) of this Will;
        2. hold one of those shares for each child of mine who died before the date that is 30 days after the date of my death and left one or more of his or her children alive on that date, and divide each deceased child’s share equally among those children of that deceased child;
      6. if Mary is not alive on the date that is 30 days after the date of my death and the trusts of paragraph 7(e) of this Will, or any of those trusts, should at any date (the “Failure Date”) fail to vest, I direct my Trustee to divide that part of my estate for which the trusts have failed, into two equal shares and:
        1. pay one of those shares to those of my father Sebastian Henry Smith, and my mother Anne Susan Smith who survive the Failure Date, and if both, equally; and
        2. pay one of those shares to those of Mary’s father Jeremiah Allan Jonston and Mary’s mother Elizabeth Mary Jonston who survive the Failure Date, and if both, equally.

      7. Powers of Trustee

    7. Trust Terms for Minors

      If any individual who becomes entitled to any part of my estate is a Minor, and I have not specified terms in this will on which my Trustee is to hold that part, I direct my Trustee to hold that part, and:

      1. pay or use so much of the income and capital of that person’s part of my estate as my Trustee decides for that person’s benefit until that person is not longer a Minor;
      2. add any income not paid or used in any year to the capital of that person’s part of my estate and then give that person what remains of that person’s part of my estate when that person reaches 19, but if that person dies before reaching 19, I direct my Trustee to give what remains of that person’s part of my estate to that person’s estate; and
      3. at any time my Trustee decides, my Trustee may give some or all of that part of my estate to that person’s parent or guardian as trustee, to receive and hold that part of my estate for that person’s benefit on the same terms set out in paragraphs (a) and (b) above.

    8. Payment to Parent or Guardian

      When my Trustee makes any payment for the benefit of any person under 19, my Trustee may make that payment to that person’s parent or guardian. When the parent or guardian receives that payment, my Trustee is discharged for that payment and need not inquire about how it is used.

    9. Convert, Keep, or Invest

      When my Trustee administers my estate:

      1. my Trustee may convert my estate or any part of my estate into money or any other form of property or security, and decide how, when, and on what terms;
      2. my Trustee may keep my estate, or any part of it, in the form it is in on the date of my death and for as long as my Trustee decides, even for the duration of the trusts in this Will. This power applies even if:
        1. the property is not an investment authorized under this Will;
        2. a debt is owing on the property; or
        3. the property does not produce income;
      3. my Trustee may invest my estate or any part of my estate in any form of property or security, whether inside or outside Canada, in which a prudent investor might invest; and
      4. if at any time my Trustee is a corporate trustee, my Trustee may invest in that corporation’s common trust funds and the corporate trustee need not account for any profit that accrues to it as a result of that investment.

    10. Allocate Assets of My Estate

      When my Trustee divides or distributes my estate, my Trustee may decide which assets of my estate (including, without limitation, money, publicly traded securities, or other property, real or personal) to allocate to any share or interest in my estate (and not necessarily equally among any shares or interests) and the value of each of those assets. Whatever value my Trustee attributes to those assets will be final and binding on everyone interested in my estate.

    11. Income Tax Elections

      1. My Trustee may, as my Trustee decides, make any allocations and elections my Trustee decides are in the best interests of my estate as a whole, including any allocations and elections under the Income Tax Act (Canada), and should consider whether my estate qualifies as a graduated rate estate under the Income Tax Act (Canada).
      2. My Trustee may make those allocations or elections, in whatever manner and proportions my Trustee decides, among the beneficiaries of my estate and any one or more of them to the exclusion of any others.
      3. Any election that allocates any portion of the income of my estate to any person for the purposes of the Income Tax Act (Canada) will not be regarded as a decision by my Trustee to allocate, in fact, that income to that person nor will that decision give that person any right to that income.
      4. My Trustee will not be considered to have breached any duty to maintain an even hand among the beneficiaries, nor will my Trustee be liable for any loss to my estate or any beneficiary of my estate as a result of any of those allocations or elections made in good faith.

    12. Deal with Business

      My Trustee, subject to any contrary provisions in this Will, may deal with any interest in any business, incorporated or otherwise, held by my estate, and may exercise any rights, powers, and privileges in connection with that interest to the same extent as I could if I were alive and the only owner of that interest.

    13. Further Powers Dealing with Property

      My Trustee, subject to any contrary provisions in this Will, may exercise any rights, powers, and privileges in connection with any property (personal or real) forming part of my estate to the same extent as I could if I were alive and the only owner of that property. My Trustee’s powers, which may be exercised how, when and on whatever terms my Trustee decides, include:

      1. selling any of that property;
      2. managing any of that property;
      3. spending money out of the capital and income, or either, to repair, convert, improve, or rebuild any of that property;
      4. razing any or all buildings on that property;
      5. erecting any building or buildings on that property;
      6. insuring any of that property against whatever risks my Trustee decides;
      7. renting, leasing, or permitting the use or occupancy of any of that property for any period of time;
      8. terminating or accepting the surrender of any lease or rental arrangement concerning that property;
      9. granting any option or right of first refusal to any person to purchase or lease any of that property for any period of time;
      10. granting, paying off or renewing any mortgages on that property;
      11. paying any person in any profession, trade, or business to transact any business or trade, or to do any act in relation to that property; and
      12. transferring that property in exchange for other property.

    19. Separate Property

      Any property to which any beneficiary becomes entitled or receives pursuant to this Will (or any trust established in it) and any property substituted for it and any income, capital gains, other increase in value or other appreciation arising from it is to be considered a gift (by way of inheritance) to that beneficiary and will at all times be his or her own private and separate property, and that property and any interest of that beneficiary in my estate, without limitation:

      1. will be excluded from an not be subject to division or distribution between that beneficiary and his or her spouse or common-law partner for any purpose, including pursuant to legislation governing the distribution of property between spouses or common-law partners in the event of death, separation or divorce or other relationship breakdown (marital or otherwise);
      2. will not be subject to any other matrimonial rights of a spouse or common-law partner of the beneficiary and will not be liable for the obligations of any spouse or common-law partner;
      3. will not form part of any community of property, partnership of acquests, family assets or property or regime of shared property to which the beneficiary may be subject;
      4. will not be considered a “family asset”, “family property”, “net family property”, “matrimonial asset”, “marital property”, or other like asset or property pursuant to legislation governing the distribution of property between spouses or common-law partners in the event of death, separation or divorce or other breakdown of a relationship (marital or otherwise); and
      5. will not be included in any calculation of that beneficiary’s net assets.

    20. No Vesting

      Unless my Trustee directs in writing, no part of the income or capital of my estate or any interest in any income or capital of my estate will vest, either absolutely or indefeasibly or subject to divestment, either in interest or possession in any beneficiary unless and until my Trustee has actually paid or applied that part of the income or capital of my estate to or for the benefit of that beneficiary and will not be subject to seizure for the payment of any debts of the beneficiary.

    21. Exoneration of Trustee

      My Trustee will not be liable for any loss or damage to my estate unless that loss or damage is caused by the actual fraud, gross negligence or wilful breach of trust on the part of my Trustee.

    22. Indemnification of Trustee

      My Trustee will be indemnified from my estate for any expenses or liability incurred by my Trustee in any legal proceeding brought by any one or more of the beneficiaries, even though the proceeding may be in respect of an alleged breach of duty by my Trustee, unless the breach results from my Trustee’s own fraud, gross negligence or wilful breach of trust.

    24. Professional Assistance and Agents

      2. may permit any person to have possession or control of all or any part of my estate;
      3. may place assets of my estate in the custody of a corporate trustee or other agent and may transfer assets to the name of that corporate trustee or other agent or any nominee of it;
      4. may pay the reasonable compensation of those professionals, agents or advisors and charge it to the income and capital of my estate in such proportions and at such times as my Trustee decides, and any compensation paid is to be taken into account and deducted from the compensation to which my Trustee would be otherwise entitled; and
